## 🎯 Project Overview

This repository documents the foundational and operational framework of the **Digital Behavior Analysis Laboratory (LACD)**. By architecture-mapping legislative texts, corporate compliance guidelines, and affirmative action policies—specifically focusing on the behavioral impact of the Brazilian Quota Law (*Lei de Cotas*)—this project bridges the gap between Behavioral Systems Analysis (BSA), Data Science (Python), and Large Language Models (LLM / Gemini API). The core objective is to audit text-based regulatory environments, identifying hidden behavioral traps, aversive control structures, and systemic bottlenecks that hinder social equity and organizational integrity.

## 1. Introduction: From Legal Code to Behavioral Contingencies

When a legal professional reviews a new statute or a corporate compliance policy, they traditionally search for statutory rights, duties, legal loopholes, and financial liabilities. However, when viewed through the lens of Organizational Behavior Management (OBM) and Behavior Analysis, these regulatory texts transform into something entirely different: **codifications of large-scale behavioral control systems**.

From a scientific standpoint, laws and organizational norms are complex networks of rule-governed behavior, forming intertwined behavioral contingencies (IBCs) and metacontingencies maintained by a controlling agency to regulate human interaction within a system. Every paragraph, subsection, and deliberate omission functions as environmental stimuli ($S^D$ or $S^{\Delta}$) that determine whether a human response will be reinforced, punished, or extinguished.

### The Problem: The Invisible Friction of Unbalanced Contingencies
Most corporate and public compliance frameworks fail because they are drafted based on formalistic legal design rather than behavioral engineering. When an organizational policy:
1. **Increases the response cost** for compliance or reporting (e.g., bureaucratic hurdles for whistleblowers).
2. **Fails to establish clear consequences** for omissions.
3. **Relies heavily on generalized aversive control** (punishment-driven rules).

The system inevitably selects dysfunctional behavioral topographies, such as operational drift, systemic anxiety, administrative ghosting, and institutional discrimination. 

The LACD was founded to solve this problem by providing a systematic, automated, and mathematically sound methodology to audit behavioral text structures using technology.

## 2. Technical Architecture & Behavioral-Digital Methodology

The LACD operates as a digital behavioral engineering pipeline. Instead of compiling code to manage computer hardware, we use code to compile, deconstruct, and audit text that manages human systems. 

### ⚙️ The Technology Stack
The laboratory environment is built entirely in a Linux ecosystem using:
* **Python 3.11+**: The core language for text processing, data orchestration, and API communication.
* **Google Gemini API (Gemini 1.5 Pro/Flash)**: Utilized as a high-context semantic parser trained via behavioral prompts to identify behavioral metrics inside textual matrices.
* **Behavioral Systems Analysis (BSA)**: The systemic framework used to map the input (laws/policies), processes (intertwined contingencies), and outcomes (cultural products/environmental impact).

### 📊 System Architecture Flow
The operational pipeline processes textual variables through three sequential, non-reversible phases:
### 🧠 Functional Mapping Design
Instead of processing syntactic or traditional legal definitions, the internal architecture forces the evaluation framework to read statutory blocks through a strict behavior-analytic lens. The system evaluates:
1. **Antecedent Stimuli ($S^D / S^{\Delta}$)** described or implied within the clauses.
2. **Behavioral Topographies** expected from target citizens or corporate agents.
3. **Consequence Networks** (schedules of reinforcement versus immediate/delayed aversive control structures).
4. **Response Cost ($R_C$)** imposed on individuals navigating the bureaucratic process.

This abstract auditing layer isolates structural "Behavioral Traps" without exposing underlying proprietary automation assets or execution code.

## 3. Case Study: Auditing the Brazilian Quota Law (Lei nº 12.711/2012)

To validate the algorithmic and behavioral framework of the LACD, we subjected the legislative amendments of the **Brazilian Quota Law (Lei de Cotas)** to a rigorous textual audit. The objective was to identify why affirmative action targets often fail to translate into stable, long-term corporate inclusion, particularly for Black women.

### 🔍 Systemic Matrix Breakdown
When the LACD parsed the statutory text, the Gemini-driven behavioral analyzer mapped out a critical imbalance between rule definitions and functional reinforcement schedules:

1. **Aversive Discriminative Stimuli ($S^D$):** The law establishes strict statistical percentages and hiring metrics. However, in the corporate environment, these function primarily as aversive control mechanisms (threat of fines, legal sanctions, and reputational damage).

![Figure 2 - Statutory Aversive Stimuli Mapping](images/statutory_stimuli_mapping.png)
   
2. **High Response Cost ($R_C$):** While the law incentivizes entry, the institutional framework within corporations drastically increases the response cost for integration. For instance, the lack of structural support, psychological safety metrics, and clear anti-discrimination operational protocols creates an environment with a high barrier to behavioral adaptation.

![Figure 3 - Corporate Response Cost Barriers](images/corporate_response_cost.png)

3. **Extinement of Prosocial Behaviors:** Because the corporate compliance mechanisms are designed around formal legal boxes rather than behavioral engineering, the target behaviors (such as proactive mentoring, promotion equity, and retention mapping) lack positive reinforcement. The system selects for *tokenism*—fulfilling the numerical quota while leaving the underlying behavioral dynamics unchanged.

### 📊 Behavioral Risk Matrix Output
The laboratory converted the textual nodes of the legislation and related corporate policies into a structured **Behavioral Risk Matrix**. The pipeline identified that the absence of explicitly codifying immediate consequences for corporate omissions generates **administrative ghosting** (leaving candidates without feedback) and exacerbates **systemic anxiety** among minority professionals.
